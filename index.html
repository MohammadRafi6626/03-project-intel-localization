1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename the newly formed company NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of innovation.

Intel starts as NM Electronics, quickly changing to Intel, and sets a standard for semiconductor excellence.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel unveils the 4004, the world's first commercially available microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution.

The 4004 enabled the creation of smaller, more efficient devices, paving the way for modern computing.

1978

8086 Microprocessor

The 8086 microprocessor is launched, establishing the x86 architecture that underpins personal computers and servers today.

The x86 platform becomes the backbone of personal and enterprise computing worldwide.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance and multitasking.

This enabled advanced software and improved efficiency in computing.

2006

Peak Greenhouse Gas Emissions

This year marks the highest annual level of greenhouse gas emissions from Intel's operations.

In the following years, Intel invests heavily in emission-reduction technologies, renewable energy, and energy-efficient manufacturing to reverse this trend.

2020

RISE Strategy Launched

Intel launches its RISE strategy (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enable) and sets ambitious 2030 goals.

RISE aims to drive industry-wide progress in climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

2022

Net Zero by 2040 Commitment

Intel commits to achieving net zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) across its global operations by 2040.

Building on years of environmental initiatives, Intel sets a new standard for the industry in climate action.

2023

Renewable Electricity Milestone

Intel achieves 99% global renewable electricity usage, significantly reducing carbon emissions.

This milestone accelerates Intel's progress towards its long-term sustainability goals.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, bringing together suppliers, government officials, and industry leaders.

Together, they collaborate on next-generation sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.
